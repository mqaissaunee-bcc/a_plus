5.1 Best Practice Troubleshooting Methodology

The 6-Step CompTIA Troubleshooting Process

1

Identify the Problem

Gather information from users, identify symptoms, determine recent changes, perform backups, and inquire about environmental or infrastructure changes.

2

Establish a Theory of Probable Cause

Question the obvious, consider multiple approaches, and divide complex problems into smaller, manageable components.

3

Test the Theory

If theory is confirmed, determine next steps. If not confirmed, establish new theory or escalate to higher-level technician.

4

Establish a Plan of Action

Create action plan to resolve the problem and identify potential effects of the solution.

5

Implement the Solution

Execute the plan or escalate as necessary. Apply fix and verify functionality.

6

Verify & Document

Verify full system functionality and implement preventive measures. Document findings, actions, and outcomes.

Which step comes AFTER testing your theory of probable cause?
A) Identify the problem
B) Establish a plan of action
C) Document findings
D) Gather information
Correct Answer: B) Establish a plan of action
After testing your theory (Step 3), the next logical step is to establish a plan of action to resolve the problem (Step 4).

5.2 Motherboard, RAM, CPU & Power Troubleshooting

Common Symptoms

POST Beep Codes

Different beep patterns indicate specific hardware failures. Consult motherboard manual for codes.

Blue Screen of Death (BSOD)

Windows crash screen often indicates memory, driver, or hardware issues.

Black Screen

No display output - could be video card, RAM, or power issue.

No Power

System won't start - check power supply, connections, and power button.

Sluggish Performance

Slow operation due to insufficient RAM, overheating, or failing components.

Overheating

High temperatures causing thermal throttling or shutdowns.

Burning Smell

Indicates component failure - immediately power off system.

Capacitor Swelling

Bulging capacitors on motherboard indicate imminent failure.

Intermittent Shutdown

Random shutdowns often caused by overheating or power issues.

Application Crashes

Frequent crashes may indicate memory problems or insufficient RAM.

Grinding Noise

Mechanical sounds from fans or hard drives indicate wear.

Inaccurate Date/Time

CMOS battery failure causing loss of system settings.

5.3 Storage Drives & RAID Arrays

Hard Drive Symptoms

Read/Write Failure

Unable to access or save files - indicates drive failure.

Slow Performance

Extended access times may indicate failing drive or fragmentation.

Loud Clicking

Mechanical drive failure - backup data immediately.

Boot Failure

System won't boot from primary drive.

Drive Not Recognized

BIOS/OS doesn't detect the drive - check connections.

Data Corruption

Files become unreadable or corrupted.

S.M.A.R.T. Errors

Self-Monitoring reports predict imminent failure.

Extended Read Times

Takes much longer to read files than normal.

RAID Array Issues

RAID 0: No redundancy - any drive failure destroys array

RAID 1: Mirror - can lose one drive without data loss

RAID 5: Requires minimum 3 drives - can lose one drive

RAID 6: Requires minimum 4 drives - can lose two drives

RAID 10: Combination of RAID 1 and 0 - high performance with redundancy

5.4 Video, Display & Projector Issues

Display Problems

VGA Mode

Low resolution indicates driver or hardware issues.

No Image on Display

Check cables, power, and video card connections.

Overheat Shutdown

Graphics card overheating causes system protection.

Dead Pixels

Individual pixels that don't change color - LCD issue.

Artifacts

Visual distortions indicating graphics card problems.

Incorrect Color Patterns

Wrong colors suggest cable or graphics issues.

Dim Display

Backlight failure in LCD monitors.

Flickering

Intermittent display suggests loose connections.

Distorted Geometry

Image size/shape issues - adjust display settings.

Burn-in

Permanent image retention on OLED displays.

Projector-Specific Issues

Dim Image

Lamp aging or dust accumulation on lens.

Color Issues

Color wheel problems or lamp spectrum shift.

Image Distortion

Keystone correction needed or lens issues.

Shutdown

Overheating protection or lamp failure.

A user reports intermittent wireless connectivity. What should you check FIRST?
A) Replace the wireless adapter
B) Check signal strength and interference
C) Reset the router
D) Update network drivers
Correct Answer: B) Check signal strength and interference
Following troubleshooting methodology, start with the most obvious and least invasive checks first. Signal strength and interference are the most common causes of intermittent wireless issues.
